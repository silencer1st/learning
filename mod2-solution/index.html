CONGENITAL ANOMALIES

PARAPHIMOSIS
refers to a retracted foreskin behind the glans penis in an uncircumcised or partially circumcised male that cannot be returned to normal position. develops venous and lymphatic congestion with severe pain, urethral constriction also acute urinary retention.
PHIMOSIS
Orifice of foreskin (prepuce) is too small / narrow to permit its normal retraction to expose the glans penis. It could be due to congenital or repeated infections commonly with scarring
EPISPADIAS
Urethra opens onto dorsal surface of penis Penopubic epispadias is most common, associated with urinary incontinence though more frequent in epispadias both types are associated with undescended testis gut malformation
